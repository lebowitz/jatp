# Julie and the Phantoms — Visual Style Guide
*Extracted from the Netflix key art / "SEPT 10" launch poster.*

---

## Color Palette
- **Foundation:** Deep midnight blue → near-black, used as the backdrop gradient (darkest at edges, lifting toward center). This is the dominant "stage void."
- **Hero glow:** Electric violet / purple as the mid-tone — the spotlight-through-fog color that frames the band.
- **Accent pop:** Hot magenta / pink, bled into the smoke. The single warm-cool tension point.
- **Warm sparks (small-area only):** Reds, oranges, golds, and silver — confined to Julie's beaded jacket and the drumsticks. These are *deliberately scarce* so they read as energy, not theme.
- **Text / highlights:** Pure white (paint-stroke) against the dark; one true red reserved for the Netflix wordmark.

**Rule:** Cool, saturated, theatrical-stage lighting. Warm color is a precious accent — never let it take over the frame.

---

## Typography
- **Logo:** Two-voice lockup. "JULIE / AND THE" in loose white **brush-script** (wet paint, energetic, hand-lettered). "PHANTOMS" in a heavier, slashing display style for contrast. The mix = handmade teen energy + rock-poster attitude.
- **Tagline:** All-caps brush script, white, same paint-stroke DNA as the logo. *"YOU ONLY LIVE ONCE, BUT YOU CAN ROCK FOREVER"* — the line itself is the thesis: mortality + music + permanence.
- **Credits / date:** Clean, wide-tracked sans-serif caps (Netflix house style) for "A NETFLIX ORIGINAL SERIES" / "SEPT 10." Quiet, utilitarian — lets the painted type carry the personality.

**Rule:** Anything with brand voice = hand-painted brush. Anything administrative = neutral sans. Don't blend the two.

---

## Composition & Imagery
- **Hierarchy:** Julie front-and-center, full-color, grounded and singing (the living anchor). The three boys float **above and behind**, mid-leap, partially dissolving into smoke — literally phantoms/holograms, lighter and more diffuse than her.
- **Motion frozen:** Everyone is caught mid-action — jump, drumstick clash, guitar swing, belted note. No static posing. Always imply the song is *happening*.
- **Smoke / fog:** Functions as the connective tissue and the "ghost" texture — the boys fade into it; Julie does not.
- **Eyeline:** Julie's eyes closed, lost in the performance (emotional sincerity); boys open-eyed, exuberant, outward (showmanship).

---

## Wardrobe Signals
- **Julie:** Maximalist, bedazzled, color-rich statement jacket (her identity = bold, reclaimed, alive).
- **Boys:** '90s rock uniform frozen in time — leather, denim, band tanks, layered tees. They literally still dress in 1995.

---

## Tone in One Line
Theatrical concert lighting + hand-painted teen-band energy + ghostly translucency = **earnest, electric, a little supernatural, never grim.**
