# Julie and the Phantoms — Story Bible & Finale Planning
*Built from Season 1 SRTs (E1–E9). For writing a Season 1 finale/continuation that resolves the cliffhanger.*

---

## THE SHOW IN ONE BREATH
Julie Molina, a teenage musician who lost her mother (and her music with her), discovers three ghosts in her mom's garage studio: the boys of **Sunset Curve**, a 1995 band who died the night of their big break (bad street-dog hot dogs). They can only be *seen* by the living when they play music together — as "Julie and the Phantoms," they become visible holograms on stage. Over the season Julie gets her music back, the boys chase the dream they lost, and a villain named Caleb Covington tries to trap them.

---

## PRINCIPAL CHARACTERS

**Julie Molina** — Lead. Singer/pianist, 15-16. Lost her mom (Rose) about a year before the series. Emotional core; warm, stubborn, funny under pressure. Her arc is grief → reopening to music → found family with the boys.

**Luke Patterson** — Lead guitarist/songwriter of Sunset Curve, d. 1995. Driving, idealistic, impulsive, all-heart. Runaway from his parents over music (huge unresolved guilt — see "Unsaid Emily"). Clear romantic chemistry with Julie (explicitly noted in-show, E7: "everyone can see the way you look at her when you sing"). Songwriting soulmate to Julie.

**Alex** — Drummer, d. 1995. Anxious, sensitive, the emotional weather-vane of the group. Gay; came out to his bandmates who accepted him. Falls for **Willie**.

**Reggie** — Bassist, d. 1995. Goofy, sweet, comic relief, secretly the most tender-hearted. Loves Julie's dad's "family energy"; country-music bit (banjo/fiddle gag).

**Bobby / "Trevor Wilson"** — The FOURTH member of Sunset Curve and the *only one who didn't die*. After the boys died, he stole their songs, claimed them as his own, and became **Trevor Wilson**, a hugely famous rockstar. He is **Carrie's father**. The boys don't learn this until E4. This betrayal is the show's biggest unresolved grievance and a prime finale thread.

**Caleb Covington** — Antagonist. Charismatic ghost showman who runs the **Hollywood Ghost Club**, an opulent afterlife venue. He "stamps" ghosts to bind their souls to him. Wants the boys under his control because they're uniquely able to be seen by the living without his help. Theatrical, seductive, menacing. Played as a song-and-dance villain.

**Willie** — A free-spirited skater ghost, bound to Caleb. Befriends and falls for Alex. Wracked with guilt for unknowingly leading the boys to Caleb. Wants to help them escape.

**Nick** — Julie's living crush/classmate, sweet and decent. Carrie's ex. **In the final scene, Caleb possesses Nick** (the cliffhanger — see below).

**Carrie** — Julie's frenemy, queen-bee pop performer (group "Dirty Candy"), Trevor/Bobby's daughter, Nick's ex. Rivalry with Julie; not evil, mostly insecure and competitive. Potential for a redemptive turn.

**Flynn** — Julie's best friend, living. Loyal, sharp, the band's unofficial hype-woman/manager. Knows the ghost secret.

**Ray Molina** — Julie's dad. Loving, goofy, photographer. Comic warmth. Doesn't know about the ghosts (thinks the band uses holograms).

**Carlos Molina** — Julie's little brother. Suspects something supernatural is going on in the house; comic subplot.

**Rose** — Julie's late mother. Wrote the songs Julie and the boys now play. Owned the studio. Her connection to the boys (she received their demo/T-shirt in E1) is a quiet thread the show seeds but never fully pays off — a possible finale resource.

## THE MAGIC RULES (as established on-screen — must stay consistent)

1. **Visibility:** The boys are invisible/intangible to the living EXCEPT when performing music with Julie — then they appear as "holograms" everyone can see. (The living rationalize them as a hologram act.)
2. **Instruments:** "Our instruments are attached to our souls" (E2) — they can play physical instruments; their gear can manifest.
3. **Poofing:** They teleport ("poof") between places.
4. **Touch:** Ghosts can't normally touch the living. The whole emotional climax of E9 is Julie suddenly being able to *feel* and hug them — a sign something has changed/strengthened.
5. **Unfinished business / crossing over:** Ghosts linger because of unfinished business. Resolve it and you "cross over" (a warm white light). The boys assumed theirs was *playing the Orpheum*. They played it (E9) — and did NOT cross over. So that wasn't it. **Open question: what IS their unfinished business?**
6. **Caleb's stamp (the curse):** Caleb stamped the boys (E5, hidden as a "club stamp" on the wrist). The stamp causes escalating painful **"jolts"** (manifesting as flickering/power-outage imagery). If they don't join his club, the jolts escalate until they **cease to exist entirely — "not anywhere"** (E7). Joining Caleb stops the jolts but binds their souls forever. Crossing over (finishing their business) would free them from the stamp altogether (E7–E8).
7. **Caleb can affect the living:** Established at the very end — he can **possess a living person** (Nick). This is new and dangerous.

## SEASON ARC (compressed)

- **E1:** 1995 — Sunset Curve dies. Present — Julie, grieving and about to be cut from her school music program, enters her mom's studio and accidentally summons the boys' ghosts via a CD. They realize 25 years have passed.
- **E2:** Julie and the boys discover that when they play together she's visible doing the impossible — a band is born. Julie reclaims her spot in the music program with "Flying Solo."
- **E3:** Willie meets Alex; the idea of "unfinished business" and crossing over is introduced. The boys think theirs is the gig they never got to play.
- **E4:** THE BIG REVEAL — the boys' old songs are now famous "Trevor Wilson" songs. Trevor is **Bobby**, their surviving bandmate who stole everything. He's Carrie's dad. Willie unknowingly leads the boys into Caleb's orbit (the Hollywood Ghost Club).
- **E5:** The Hollywood Ghost Club. Caleb performs ("seductive recruiter" energy), and secretly **stamps** the boys.
- **E6:** Carrie/Dirty Candy rivalry; Julie's world (school, Nick, family) deepens. The boys grapple with the Trevor betrayal and their fading relevance.
- **E7:** The jolts worsen. Willie reveals the truth: the stamp will destroy them unless they join Caleb — OR find and finish their unfinished business to cross over and break free. Carrie grows suspicious of the "holograms."
- **E8:** Race against the curse. The boys debate sacrificing the band (and Julie) to save themselves vs. risking nonexistence. Emotional low point; "Unsaid Emily" territory (Luke's parents). They decide the band — being with Julie — matters more than self-preservation.
- **E9 (finale):** Caleb pressures them. The boys are offered the Orpheum (their dream stage) via Willie. They play the Orpheum with Julie ("Stand Tall") — triumphant — but do NOT cross over, proving the Orpheum was never their true unfinished business. The jolts have nearly destroyed them; they're fading. In the studio after, Julie discovers she can suddenly **touch/hug them** (a mysterious new strength). Joy. THEN —

## THE CLIFFHANGER (exact final beats, verbatim from E9)

In the studio, the band is whole and euphoric:
- "We played the Orpheum!" / "Whoo!" / "Yeah, we did!"

Hard cut to Nick (Julie's crush), alone outside. Caleb appears to him:
- CALEB: "How sweet. The broken-hearted teenager fighting for his girl."
- NICK: "Do I know you?"
- CALEB: "You will."
- (Caleb's stamp glows — he **possesses Nick**.)
- NICK/CALEB: "I have a fight too."
- Then, smiling at the camera with Caleb behind the eyes: "Hi, Nick!" [text on screen / a wink to the audience]
- Final word, chilling: "Thanks."

**The unresolved hooks going into a hypothetical Season 2 / the finale we're writing:**
1. **The stamp curse is NOT broken.** The boys still bear Caleb's stamp. Crossing over (true unfinished business) is the only escape, and they still haven't identified it. Why are they suddenly stronger / why can Julie touch them?
2. **Caleb has possessed Nick** — he can now act through the living and is targeting Julie's world directly.
3. **Julie can touch the ghosts now** — a rule-change that begs explanation (love? a deepening bond? a step toward them becoming "real"? a side effect of nearly crossing over?).
4. **The Trevor/Bobby betrayal is unavenged and unresolved** — the boys' stolen legacy.
5. **Luke & Julie's romance** is unconsummated and complicated by the living/dead divide.
6. **Alex & Willie** — Willie is still bound to Caleb; their relationship is unresolved.
7. **Rose's connection** to the boys (she got their demo in 1995) is a dangling thread that could become meaningful.

## VOICE / TONE NOTES (for script fidelity)

- **Register:** Disney/Netflix family musical. PG. Earnest, warm, funny. Real emotional stakes (grief, mortality, found family) handled sincerely but never grim.
- **Music is structural:** Songs are emotional turning points, not decoration. A finale needs at least 1–2 musical numbers at its climaxes. (Existing song style: pop-rock anthems, big choruses, "stand tall / wake up / now or never" uplift vocabulary.)
- **Comedy beats:** Reggie is the primary comic engine (dim-sweet non-sequiturs, country-music bit, loves Ray). Alex's anxiety is mined for nervous comedy. Carlos and Ray provide domestic comedy. Banter is rapid, overlapping, affectionate, full of interrupting and finishing each other's sentences (see E4 "Rock 'n' Roll Hall of Fame" riff).
- **Luke's voice:** intense, dreamer, "I want that connection with everybody" — speaks in mission statements.
- **Julie's voice:** grounded, wry, emotionally honest; she punctures the boys' nonsense but is the heart.
- **Caleb's voice:** theatrical impresario, velvet menace, speaks in showman flourishes and veiled threats ("You will.").
- **Willie's voice:** chill skater warmth, guilt under the surface.
- **Catchphrase texture:** "poof out," "lifers" (Caleb's term for the living), "crossed over," "the Orpheum," "Sunset Curve."

## CONTINUITY GUARDRAILS (don't break these when writing the finale)

- The boys cannot be touched by/touch the living — EXCEPT the new development with Julie at the end of E9. Any expansion of this must be treated as a special, escalating mystery, not a casual ability.
- The living see the boys ONLY when they perform. Off stage, only Julie (and the audience) experience them. (Carlos suspects; Flynn knows because Julie told her.)
- Caleb is powerful but his power has rules: stamps, the club, possession. He's not omnipotent — he's threatened by the boys' independent visibility.
- Trevor/Bobby is human and famous; he can't see the ghosts. Carrie can't either.
- Deaths/timeline: boys died 1995, it's ~2020. 25 years.
